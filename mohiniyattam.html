Mohiniyattam

Introduction to Mohiniyattam

Mohiniyattam, also known as the dance of the enchantress, is a classical dance form from Kerala, India. It is one of the eight recognized Indian classical dance forms, characterized by graceful, swaying body movements meant to embody the lyrical movements and feminine moods. The name Mohiniyattam comes from the words "Mohini" (meaning a woman who enchants onlookers) and "attam" (meaning dance). Thus, Mohiniyattam essentially means 'dance of the enchantress.'

Historical Roots

This dance form, which is said to have originated in the 16th century, was traditionally a solo dance performed by women and expressed Hindu religious themes and spiritual ideas, particularly of the Vaishnavism tradition involving Vishnu as Mohini. Its roots are also tied with the ancient history of Kerala, and the dance was systematized in the 18th century, with a significant revival and restructuring in the 20th century.

Performance and Techniques

Mohiniyattam is performed solo, with the dancer wearing a simple white sari embroidered with golden brocade at the edges, symbolizing the traditional Kerala attire. The performance is marked by graceful gestures and footwork that follow the sopana style of singing. This style is characterized by a slow melodic tempo, with lyrics that are generally in Malayalam.

The dance integrates elements of a classical style called 'adavus' and improvisational dance called 'abhinaya', which focuses on expressive acting. Dancers use subtle facial expressions to convey complex emotions, narrating stories from Hindu epics. The music typically used in Mohiniyattam involves the melodic instrument Veena, along with the Maddalam (a type of drum), Idakka, and Flute.

Cultural Significance

Mohiniyattam holds a unique place in the cultural and spiritual tapestry of India, with performances that can be seen in temples, during traditional festivals, and modern stage shows. This dance form is not only a medium of storytelling but also a spiritual expression that seeks to communicate feminine love, devotion, and a spectrum of human emotions through refined gestures and footwork.
